# iot_foosball
Project



It’s late evening in Tartu, and I walk into the student lounge where the foosball table is softly glowing, almost inviting me to play. As I approach, the screen lights up and recognizes me, showing my stats and welcoming me back, which already makes the experience feel personal rather than just a casual game. My friend joins, we tap our cards, and the match begins. Instantly, the table comes alive—scores update automatically, and when a goal is scored, bright lights flash and a sound plays, removing any arguments about whether it counted. At the same time, there are simple control buttons on the side that let us manually adjust the score if needed, giving us control in case something isn’t detected correctly, and another button releases the ball to start or restart play, making the whole experience feel smooth and intentional rather than chaotic.
